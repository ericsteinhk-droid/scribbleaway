================================================================================
NMS / DDN BILINGUAL LEXICON AND STYLE GUIDE (EN <> FR)
Reference for assisting automated translation of technical specifications
================================================================================

SOURCE BASIS
  Derived from a comparison of 5 matched English/French section pairs of the
  Canadian National Master Construction Specification (NMS) / Devis directeur
  national de la construction au Canada (DDN):
    - 01 33 00  Submittal Procedures / Documents-échantillons à soumettre (2009-12-31)
    - 03 45 00  Precast Architectural Concrete / Éléments préfabriqués en
                béton architectural (2019-11-06)
    - 04 05 00  Common Work Results for Masonry / Maçonnerie - exigences
                générales concernant les résultats des travaux (2023-11-06)
    - 06 10 53  Miscellaneous Rough Carpentry / Charpenterie diverse (2021-11-05)
    - 07 21 13  Board Insulation / Isolant en panneaux (2022-04-20)

  These observations reflect only the 5 sampled sections. Vintage matters: the
  2009 section (01 33 00) uses slightly older phrasing and capitalized defined
  terms ("Documents Contractuels", "Entrepreneur"); the 2019-2023 sections use
  lower-case ("documents contractuels") and added sustainability content. Where
  a convention is not consistent across all samples, that is noted. Treat
  anything uncertain as a candidate to verify against a current MasterFormat /
  NMS source rather than as a fixed rule.

================================================================================
PART 1 - STRUCTURAL / EDITORIAL CONVENTIONS (highest priority for consistency)
================================================================================

1.1  THREE-PART SECTION STRUCTURE (PART headings)
  Every section follows the CSC/MasterFormat three-part model. Translate the
  PART headings exactly as follows; these are fixed:
    GENERAL    ->  GÉNÉRALITÉS
    PRODUCTS   ->  PRODUITS
    EXECUTION  ->  EXÉCUTION
  When a part is empty, "NOT USED" -> "SANS OBJET" (NMS standard; not present in
  the 2009 sample, which left the French headings absent - verify per template).

1.2  EDITOR NOTES (the single most distinctive NMS/DDN convention)
  English editor notes are tagged:
       **SPEC NOTE: **              ->  **NOTE AU RÉDACTEUR : **
       **SPEC NOTE DESCRIPTION: **  ->  **NOTE AU RÉDACTEUR - DESCRIPTION : **
  Notes:
    - In the older 2009 section the French tag appears as "RÉDACTEUR :" alone;
      the modern standard is "NOTE AU RÉDACTEUR". Prefer the modern form.
    - French inserts a space before the colon (": " becomes " : ") per French
      typographic rule. This applies to ALL colons in the French text, not just
      editor notes (e.g. "Qualifications :", "Échantillons :").
    - Editor notes are instructions to the spec writer, NOT contract text. They
      are never numbered and are stripped from the issued project manual. Keep
      them clearly distinct; do not fold them into the article body.

1.3  ARTICLE NUMBERING AND PARAGRAPH SCHEME
    - Articles (## level) are numbered 1.01, 1.02 ... in PART 1; 2.01 ... in
      PART 2; 3.01 ... in PART 3. The number is identical in both languages;
      only the title is translated.
    - Paragraph levels use the NMS dotted scheme: .1, .2, .3 then nested .1, .2.
      This scheme is language-independent - preserve numbering exactly.
    - The French extraction shows the leading ".1 / .2" markers explicitly; the
      English may render them as flat lines. Do not renumber when translating;
      map paragraph-for-paragraph.

1.4  PLACEHOLDERS AND OPTIONAL TEXT (preserve verbatim)
    - Fill-in blanks: [______]  -> keep identical (same bracket + underscores).
    - Bracketed optional/choice text: [exterior] [interior] -> [extérieur]
      [intérieur]. Brackets denote writer choices and MUST be preserved around
      the corresponding translated span, including nested brackets
      e.g. [[complete] [standard]] -> [[complète] [standard]].
    - Numeric values, units and dimensions are NOT translated: 100 x 100 mm,
      [24] hours -> [24] heures, 19% , kg/m3, L/m2, MPa, mm all stay.
      French uses a comma decimal: 0.15 mm -> 0,15 mm. Watch this conversion.

1.5  HEADER / TITLE BLOCK
    CANADIAN NATIONAL MASTER CONSTRUCTION SPECIFICATION
        -> DEVIS DIRECTEUR DE LA CONSTRUCTION AU CANADA
    SECTION xx xx xx   -> SECTION xx xx xx  (unchanged)
    PAGE __ OF __      -> PAGE __ DE __
    Date format YYYY-MM-DD is unchanged.

1.6  REFERENCE STANDARDS - DO NOT TRANSLATE STANDARD TITLES
    The titles of cited standards (ASTM, CSA, ANSI, ISO, NLGA, ASME ...) remain
    in English in BOTH versions. Example, unchanged in the French text:
      ASTM C578-[19] Standard Specification for Rigid, Cellular Polystyrene
      Thermal Insulation
    Only the NAMES OF STANDARDS BODIES and any descriptive Canadian-code titles
    are translated (see Part 2.7). Standard designations/numbers never change.

PART 3 - STYLISTIC RULES (verb mood, register, recurring patterns)
================================================================================

3.1  IMPERATIVE / INFINITIVE VERB MOOD
  This is the defining stylistic feature. English specs use the bare imperative
  ("Submit", "Install", "Verify", "Do not proceed"). French DDN uses the
  INFINITIVE, not the imperative ("Soumettre", "Poser/Installer", "S'assurer",
  "Ne pas entreprendre"). Map every English instruction verb to a French
  infinitive. Common pairs observed:
    Submit            -> Soumettre
    Install / Apply   -> Poser / Installer / Appliquer
    Provide           -> Fournir / Prescrire (when specifying)
    Verify / Ensure   -> S'assurer / Vérifier / Veiller à ce que
    Review            -> Examiner / Revoir
    Inspect           -> Inspecter
    Coordinate        -> Coordonner
    Indicate          -> Indiquer
    Keep / Store      -> Conserver / Garder / Entreposer
    Cut and trim      -> Découper et tailler
    Fit (tight)       -> Ajuster / Abouter (joints)
    Do not proceed    -> Ne pas entreprendre / Ne pas procéder
    Refer to / See    -> Se reporter à / Voir
    Allow [X] days    -> Laisser [X] jours
    Notify / Inform   -> Aviser / Informer
    Obtain            -> Obtenir
    Offset (joints)   -> Décaler
    Embed             -> Noyer
    Secure / Attach   -> Assujettir / Fixer
    Extend            -> Poser ... (extend boards) / prolonger
    Overlap           -> Faire chevaucher / chevaucher

3.2  REGISTER AND OMISSION OF ARTICLES (English telegraphic style)
  English NMS drops articles for brevity: "Submit shop drawings", "Keep
  materials dry until use", "Store materials off ground". French restores normal
  grammar with articles: "Soumettre les dessins d'atelier", "Garder les
  matériaux secs jusqu'à leur utilisation", "Entreposer les matériaux ... de
  manière qu'ils ne reposent pas sur le sol". Do not carry the article-dropping
  into French; produce grammatical French.

3.3  TYPOGRAPHY
    - French space before high punctuation: " : ", and guillemets with spaces:
      « C » , « dessins d'atelier ». English uses straight quotes: "shop drawings".
    - Decimal comma in French (0,15 mm), decimal point in English (0.15 mm).
    - Multiplication sign: English "100 x 100 mm"; French commonly "100 × 100 mm"
      (true multiplication sign appears in the masonry sample).
    - Apostrophes: French uses the typographic right single quote (U+2019).

3.4  DEFINED-TERM CAPITALIZATION
  Older sections capitalize contract-defined nouns in French (Entrepreneur,
  Documents Contractuels, Représentant du Ministère). Newer sections lowercase
  most (entrepreneur, documents contractuels) but keep the bracketed role
  placeholders capitalized: [Représentant du Ministère]. FLAG: match the
  capitalization style of the specific section's vintage; do not normalize
  across sections without instruction.

3.5  "WORK" - travaux vs ouvrage
  "Work" (the contractual scope/activity) -> "travaux".
  "Work" (a physical built result / portion) -> "ouvrage".
  Watch context: "portion of Work" = "partie de l'ouvrage"; "delay in Work" =
  "retarder l'exécution des travaux"; "finished Work" tolerances often "ouvrages
  finis".

3.6  CROSS-REFERENCE FORMAT
  "Section 03 30 00 - Cast-in-Place Concrete" -> "section 03 30 00 - Béton
  coulé en place". The number is identical; only the descriptive title is
  translated; the en-dash/space separator is kept. In French "section" is
  usually lowercase mid-sentence; "Section" capitalized at line start of a list.

3.7  KNOWN OCR/SOURCE ARTIFACTS TO IGNORE (do not propagate into translations)
  The English samples contain spec-writer typos that should be silently
  corrected, not mirrored, in any cleaned bilingual output:
    "oOffset" (07 21 13)  -> intended "Offset"
    "méralliques" (FR 06 10 53) -> intended "métalliques"
    "before to proceeding" (01 33 00) -> intended "before proceeding"
  These confirm the source is a working master, not a copy-edited final.

PART 4 - USAGE NOTES FOR AUTOMATED TRANSLATION
================================================================================
  1. Translate structure and boilerplate from the fixed maps in Part 1-2 first;
     these recur identically across thousands of NMS paragraphs and are the
     highest-leverage consistency wins.
  2. Never translate: standard designations/numbers, section numbers, units,
     numeric values, [______] blanks, bracket structure.
  3. Always convert: verb mood (imperative -> infinitive), decimal separator,
     colon spacing, quote style, and restore French articles.
  4. Flag for human review: EPD/FDE/DEP choice, "cradle to gate" rendering,
     capitalization of defined terms (vintage-dependent), and any term carrying
     a "verify" note above.
  5. This lexicon reflects 5 sampled sections only. It is a starting reference,
     not an authoritative termbase. For production use, validate against the
     current official NMS/DDN release and Termium Plus / the PSPC/SPAC
     terminology resources.
