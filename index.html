Profile

A dedication to
the enduring

Éric Stein is an Associate Director and Senior Heritage Conservation Architect at EVOQ Architecture, one of Canada's foremost multidisciplinary firms. A graduate of McGill University's School of Architecture (1991), he joined EVOQ in 2001 and was named Associate in 2009 and Director in 2022.

With professional experience spanning over three decades, Éric brings wide-ranging expertise in institutional, commercial, and residential projects of all scales. His technical mastery and methodical project management approach allow him to lead heritage restorations and new construction from pre-design through construction contract administration — navigating the most complex challenges with confidence.

An in-depth knowledge of traditional building materials and techniques underpins Éric's particular focus on the rehabilitation of 19th and 20th century building envelopes, with a deep interest in religious architecture. His passion for photography adds a documentary dimension to each project, capturing the essence and transformation of heritage sites over time.

Expertise

Domains of practice

Heritage Conservation

Comprehensive conservation and rehabilitation of 19th and 20th century structures. Deep familiarity with traditional materials, historic masonry, traditional roofing systems, and decorative metalwork — applied through rigorous research and testing.

Building Envelope Rehabilitation

Specialized expertise in the restoration and upgrading of historic building envelopes: masonry, stone, copper roofing, glazing systems, and ornamental elements — balancing authenticity with contemporary performance requirements.

Religious Architecture

A particular interest and sustained practice in religious heritage, including sensitive interventions at cathedrals, churches, and grand seminaries. Active member of the Modern Religious Heritage Committee, MCCQ.

Project Management

Full-cycle project leadership from pre-design through construction administration. Proven capacity to coordinate complex, multi-stakeholder heritage mandates involving government bodies, preservation authorities, and specialized consultants.

Institutional & Civic Architecture

Extensive experience with federal, provincial, and municipal heritage landmarks — parliamentary buildings, courthouses, city halls, and cultural institutions — managing restoration to national heritage standards.

Heritage Documentation

A complementary practice of architectural photography and documentary work that informs and enriches heritage analysis, providing a rigorous visual record of existing conditions and the transformation of historic sites.

Selected Work

Projects

01

Wellington Building

Heritage Conservation Architect for the rehabilitation of this federal government building within Ottawa's Parliamentary Precinct. Scope included copper roof replacement, restoration of masonry envelope, all 428 exterior windows, ornamental glass entrance canopy, and historic interior spaces — mosaic-vaulted vestibule, marble lobbies, and heritage stairs. Recipient of a CAHP Award of Excellence.

Heritage Rehabilitation Ottawa, Ontario
02

Historic City Hall

Exterior and site rehabilitation of Calgary's iconic civic landmark, a national, provincial, and municipal heritage property. In partnership with Lemay as architect of record, the mandate revitalizes the building's envelope and civic presence, designed to serve Calgary for another century.

Heritage Restoration Calgary, Alberta
03

Grand Séminaire de Montréal

Master plan for one of Montreal's most significant heritage ensembles — a complex of buildings and landscapes spanning several centuries of construction. The plan establishes a long-term conservation and development framework for this exceptional religious and educational site.

Heritage Master Plan Montréal, Québec
04

Elgin County Courthouse

Project management for the restoration of traditional roofing systems and exterior masonry at this significant Ontario heritage building. Rigorous material analysis and specialist contractor coordination ensured the work met heritage conservation standards.

Heritage Restoration St. Thomas, Ontario
05

F.A.C.E. School Auditorium

Restoration and contemporary upgrading of the auditorium at this Montreal institution, integrating modern performance and technical requirements within a heritage context.

Heritage & Contemporary Montréal, Québec
06

Nicolet Cathedral

Sensitive conservation interventions at this significant Québec religious heritage landmark. The work exemplifies the careful, evidence-based approach to sacred heritage that defines Éric Stein's practice.

Religious Heritage Nicolet, Québec
